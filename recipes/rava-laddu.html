Rava Laddu

"sweet ball" by dhyanji is licensed under CC BY-NC-ND 2.0 .

Description

Rava Laddu is a South Indian dessert made for gifting during festivals. It is made from semolina and ghee into little balls that melt in the mouth.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup semolina/suji rava
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 4 tbsp dessicated cocnut
  • 1/4 cup ghee
  • 1 tsp cardamom powder
  • 10 raisins
  • 10 cashews, broken

Steps

  1. In a dry pan, roast semolina and dessicated coconut on a low flame for 5 minutes while constantly stirring.
  2. When it starts to give off an aroma without browning, remove from heat and let it cool.
  3. Blend sugar and cardamom into a fine powder.
  4. Add the semolina mixture to the blender and grind into a slightly coarse powder. Transfer into a mixing bowl.
  5. Heat the pan with ghee and fry cashews and raisins until golden.
  6. Pour this ghee-nut mixture into the mixing bowl and stir well by hand.
  7. Divide the mixture into small fist-sized portions and roll hard with fingers to form a ball.
  8. If the mixture is too dry, add ghee until laddus can be made this way.
  9. Store the laddus in an airtight container at in a cool and dry place at room temperature for 10-14 days.
